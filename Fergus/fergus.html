Surgical Application

Using AI to help reduce surgical complications

According to the World Health Organization, of the many millions of people who undergo surgical treatment, 25% of patients experience surgical complications, with the crude mortality rate of 0.5-5%. Although according to Ryan Patrick Jones/CBC News, actions taken by surgeons are not the sole cause of these surgical complications, it is still one of the major causes of these complications. And Dr. Armin Madani, a general surgeon with the Sprott Department of Surgery at the University Health Network in Toronto wants to reduce this risk.

When he was researching the techniques and thought processes adopted by world-class surgeons, a group of data and computer scientists proposed using Artificial intelligence (AI) to mimic their minds.

The product of the collaboration is a prototype with the use of computer vision the ability to identify whether an organ is safe to dissect or not in real-time,
