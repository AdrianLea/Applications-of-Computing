Surgical Application

Using AI to help reduce surgical complications

According to the World Health Organization , of the many millions of people undergo surgical treatment, 25% of patients experience surgical complications, with the crude mortality rate of 0.5-5%. Although according to Ryan Patrick Jones/CBC News, action taken by surgeons are not the sole cause of these surgical complications, it is still one of the major cause of these complications.And Dr. Armin Madani, a general surgeon with the Sprott Department of Surgery at University Health Network in toronto wants to reduce this risk.

When he was researching the techinques and thought process adaopted by world-class surgeons, a group of data and computer scientist proposed the use of Artifical Intellegence(AI) to mimic their mind.

A prototype with the use of computer vision with the ability of to identify whether a organ is safe to remove or not in real time, is produced with the collaboration
